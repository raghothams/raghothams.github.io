Sharing Knowledge

This post is mostly the thoughts that were triggered when I saw this tweet from James Clear -

Unshared knowledge is like potential energy. It’s nice to have, but it only changes things when you turn it into something else. Your legacy is what you share, not what you know. https://t.co/0mq82fQUnP

— James Clear (@JamesClear) January 5, 2020

I have always had the habit of sharing everything I learn, be it a simple tweet or a complex concept. I derive immense happiness from sharing what I learn and it increases multi-fold when you see the other person having an impact from the learnings.

I had a realization that most people hold on to knowledge acquired just so that they can be superior beings. Toxic culture at workplaces show how team perform reduces drastically when voluntary knowledge sharing isn't an integral part of them.

People are what they are mostly because of the people around them. We have largely influenced by people in our first two circles of relation. In my case, each of them share learnings with completely no inhibition. Everyone comes from diverse backgrounds, interests and that's what make our conversations beautiful.

“Creativity is just connecting things. When you ask creative people how they did something, they feel a little guilty because they didn’t really do it, they just saw something. It seemed obvious to them after a while.” Steve Jobs
